Brazil's unemployment rate in July was 5.58 percent, down from 5.92 percent in June, the National Statistics Institute (IBGE) said. This was, however, up from unemployment of 4.83 percent in July last year.   The largest increase in the number of people employed was seen in the construction civil sector, with a rise of 2.1 percent or 25,000 people. Average real income of employed people increased approximately seven percent from June 1995 to June 1996, IBGE said. -- Fatima Cristina, Sao Paulo newsroom, 55-21-5074151
