Sri Lankan stocks closed firmer on Thursday, with trade volume rising sharply for selected shares, brokers said. The Colombo Stock Exchange all share index rose 2.85 points to 559.25 from its previous close on Tuesday. The market was closed on Wednesday for a Buddhist holiday. Turnover, supported by more than 500,000 Kandy Hotels shares which changed hands in pre-arranged deals, was 29.4 million rupees against 6.2 million on Tuesday. Kandy closed at 34 rupees, up 1.75. Associated Motorways, which was also actively traded, fell 4.75 to 34.75.
