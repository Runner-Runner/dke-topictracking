Indian copper prices fell while nickel, tin and lead gained on Thursday, dealers said. Ready copper fell by 100 rupees to 12,500 rupees per quintal on fresh offerings following expectations of a price cut to be announced by Hindustan Copper Ltd., they said. Tin, nickel and lead on the other hand climbed on restricted supplies and fresh support on reports of a recovery in the world market, one dealer said. Tin rose by 400 rupees to 36,000 rupees, nickel gained by 200 to 38,700 rupees and lead was up 100 to 4,900 rupees. Aluminium on the other hand remained quiet at 7,450 rupees, so was zinc at 6,300 rupees on nominal trade. -- Bombay Commodities +91-22-265 9000
