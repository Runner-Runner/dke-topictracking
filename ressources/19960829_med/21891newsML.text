U.S. factories used 32.5 million lbs of lard in edible and inedible product output in July 1996 versus a revised 31.4 million lbs in June and 34.9 million lbs in July 1995 the Census Bureau said. Usage so far in the 1996 period amounted to 249.2 million lbs versus 271.1 million lbs in the 1995 period. Usage includes hydrogenated vegetable and animal fats and other fats and oils in process.
