The State (central) Bank of Pakistan (SBP) depreciated the rupee on Thursday to 35.6200/35.8337 to the dollar from 35.5300/35.7077 fixed on August 13, the bank said.   The bank gave no reason for the depreciation, the latest in a series carried out under the central bank's "crawling peg" management of the embattled local currency. The rupee has fallen 0.85 percent this month and 1.48 percent since July 1, the start of fiscal 1996/97. It has lost four percent of its dollar value since January 1. -- Karachi newsroom 9221-5685192
