Singapore government bonds ended lower on Thursday in moderate trade on interbank and retail interest, dealers said. "Bond prices and the interbank rate were marked down very slightly today. Treasury bill yields ended slightly higher and volume was moderate," a local dealer said. In treasury bills, three-months closed at a yield of 1.28/23 percent compared with Wednesday's yield of 1.24/19 percent. One-year rose to 1.36/31 percent from Wednesday's yield of 1.35/30 percent.   Among bonds, July 1998 rose to S$102.35/45 from Wednesday's close of S$102.33/43. March 2001 eased to S$99.70/80 from Wednesday's close of S$99.75/85. June 2003 eased to S$99.51/56 from Wednesday's close of S$99.52/57. The overnight interbank offered rate eased to 3-1/2 3-3/8 percent from Wednesday's close of 3-5/8 3-1/2 percent. -- Singapore newsroom (65-870-3080)
