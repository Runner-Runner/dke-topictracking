The U.S. Agriculture Department said Thursday it would donate 3,500 tonnes of cotton to the Agricultural Foundation to assist rural development in Poland. Plans called for selling the cotton to privately run mills in Poland. Proceeds would provide assistance to private farmers and businesses in rural areas. A portion also would be useds for humanitarian aid. The donation was made under USDA's Food for Progress program, which aids free-market reforms. The donations will be carried out before the September 30 end of fiscal 1996.
