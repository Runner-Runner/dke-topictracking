Hyundai Engineering and Construciton, a unit of South Korea's Hyundai Group, said on Thursday it won a $480 million power plant construction order from Ekran Bhd of Malaysia.   A Hyundai spokesman said the company would build a 205-metre-high concrete dam and install 165-km-long cables for the Bakun Water Power Plant in Malaysia's Sarawak state. He said construction of the Bakun plant would cost a total of $5.1 billion. Construction will begin in September and with completion by the year 2003. Hyundai has won a total of $2.6 billion worth of construction orders from Malaysia, including the power plant order. -- Seoul Newsroom (822) 727 5647
