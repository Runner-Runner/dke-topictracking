Spot electricity prices in the northeastern U.S. were steady to lower Thursday amid cooler-than-normal summer weather in the region, industry sources said. The latest weather forecasts indicated temperatures in the Northeast to be normal to above-normal next week. Trading of daily power in the region was subdued ahead of the three-day U.S. Labor Day weekend, traders said. In the New York Power Pool (NYPP), peak electricity prices for next-day delivery were quoted at $23.00-$24.00 per megawatt hour (MWh), near unchanged from earlier this week. Next-day, off-peak power in NYPP was assessed at $14.00-$16.00, down from $18.00-$22.50 earlier this week. In the New England Power Pool (NEPOOL), peak power for Friday delivery was discussed at $24.00-$25.00, down from the $25.00-$27.00 range for the early part of the week. Next-day NEPOOL prices for off-peak hours were pegged at $17.00-$18.00, compared with $18.00-$22.50 earlier this week. --R Leong, New York Power Desk +1 212 859 1622
