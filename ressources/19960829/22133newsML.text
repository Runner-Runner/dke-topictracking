Discount broker Lombard Brokerage Inc said it was cutting fees for trading on the internet. Lombard said that, under a new schedule to be effective September 16, it will charge customers $14.95 to trade up to 5,000 shares for stocks listed on the New York and American Stock Exchanges, as well as over-the-counter stocks. Limit orders will cost $19.95 for up to 5,000 shares of listed and O-T-C stocks. Lombard said it will charge $0.01 per share on trades of more than 5,000 shares. The firm will eliminate a $2.50 per order handling fee, also effective September 16. The fee for broker-assisted orders will remain at $34.00 per transaction.
