Curtis Mathes Holding Corp said on Thursday it planned to recognize a $1.2 million gain in the first quarter of fiscal 1997. In a statement, the company reported a loss of $5.9 million, or $0.35 a share, on revenues of 7.7 million in the 1996 financial year ended June 30. The previous financial year Curtis Mathes reported a loss of $4.2 million, or $0.45 a share, on revenues of $21.3 million. "Interest expense for fiscal 1996 decreased by $991,000 from 1995, which is primarily attributable to the settlement of the company's line of credit and related notes payable ...," the statement said. "As a result of the renegotiation of debt, the company plans to recognize a $1.2 million gain during the first quarter of 1997. The company also expects to be released from a $1.5 million off-balance sheet risk associated with this renegotiation of debt." -- New York newsroom, (212) 859-1610
