His rival, ousted leader Megawati Sukarnoputri, is said to be facing further questioning, although this time by the Attorney-General's office and not the police. About 2,000 Timor cars made by KIA Motors of South Korea are due to arrive at Jakarta's Tanjung Priok port on Thursday. The cars are the first of around 40,000 of the foreign-made vehicles being imported under Indonesia's national car policy which is designed to promote local components industries.
