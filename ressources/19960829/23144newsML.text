AT&T of the United States launched on Thursday its first switched digital international (SDI) and global software defined network (GSDN) services in Indonesia. "We believe the launch of our new SDI and GSDN services will make the use of high-speed digital technology for quality transmission a more attractive proposition for companies all over the world," Donald B. Westmore, AT&T Asia Pacific country manager for Indonesia, said in a statement. SDI service is a dial-up, digital service that provides reliable, high quality and high capacity transmission of data, graphics, video, voice and audio. GSDN is a high quality international virtual networking service which provides integrated worldwide connectivity for voice, fax and data through point-to-point, two-way voice and voice-based communication. -- Jakarta newsroom +6221 384-6364
