China M0 money supply, or cash in circulation, rose 10.1 percent year-on-year to the end of July to reach 780.9 billion yuan ($94.1 billion), the Economic Information Daily said on Thursday. It gave no further details. Last week, central bank governor Dai Xianglong said China's M1 -- currency in circulation plus current business deposits -- rose in July by 15 percent over a year earlier. Broad M2 money supply rose a year-on-year 27 percent, Dai had said. ($1 = 8.3 yuan)
