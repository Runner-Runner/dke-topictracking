The Washington Post carried the following stories on its front page on August 29: --- CHICAGO - Democrats prepared to renominate Bill Clinton for a second run at the White House. --- CHICAGO - Organised labour is ready to challenge Republicans in their suburban districts and the Christian Coalition in the churches. --- WASHINGTON - Bill Clinton's tenure in the White House reflects his mercurial career with its unforgettable highs and lows. --- WASHINGTON - Metro officials knew last November that there were problems rail cars' brakes, but did not correct them until after the January crash that killed a train operator. --- WASHINGTON - The U.S. government has a plan to use bomb-sniffing dogs to improve security at up to 50 of the nation's biggest airports. --- JERUSALEM - Palestinian leader Yasser Arafat lashed out at the Israeli government and accused its leaders of "declaring a state of war against the Palestinian people."
