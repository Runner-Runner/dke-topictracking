High cotton prices have triggered another special quota permitting entry of 44,403,388 kilograms (97,892,793 lbs), the U.S. Department of Agriculture said Thursday. The quota, being established on Nov. 23, 1996, applies to cotton purchased not later than Feb. 20, 1997 and entered into the United States not later than May 21, 1997, USDA said. The quota amount is for the period May 1996 through July 1996.
