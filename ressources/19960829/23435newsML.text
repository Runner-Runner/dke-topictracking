Credit Foncier de France, the troubled property lender which is due to be rescued in a bid by state-owned Caisse des Depots et de Consignations, is set to announce a first half profit on Thursday. The L'Agefi newspaper said the profit will be between 200 million to 400 million francs, against an expected loss of 520 million. L'Agefi said the profit could have consequences for the Caisse bid of 70 francs per share, which is currently being studied for its fairness by an independent expert. Credit Foncier was suspended from trading on Wednesday. ($1 = 5.06 francs)   According to L'Agefi, Credit Foncier could now be worth up to between 95 and 100 francs per share. -- Paris newsroom +33 1 4221 5452
