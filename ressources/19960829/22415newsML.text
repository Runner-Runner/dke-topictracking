The tone of several markets in Europe improved slightly as mills returning from annual holidays sought to cover their essential autumn requirements, industry sources said. Buying interest, albeit rather selective, dealt mainly with central Asian and African franc zone medium/better qualities. In contrast, Eastern Europe was rather slow and in the long staple sector, fine spinners were again content to await the start of Egyptian new crop marketing before anticipating winter shipment needs. --London Newsroom +44 171 542 8074
