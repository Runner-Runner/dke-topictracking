Chrysler Corp said its U.S. sales for the 1996 model year have set a record with more than a month remaining in the model year. Chrysler said its dealers sold 2,189,420 new Chrysler, Plymouth, Dodge, Jeep and Eagle vehicles in the United States from October 1, 1995, through August 28, 1996.  The previous record for a full model year -- October 1 through September 30 -- was 1994's 2,186,128. Chrysler's sales for the model year to date are running 11 percent ahead of a year ago. Its share of the U.S. market for the model year to date is 15.8 percent, 1.7 percentage points higher than last year.
